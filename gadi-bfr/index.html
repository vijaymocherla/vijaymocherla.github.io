"The Financial Market is a very emotional place that appeals to fear and greed"

-Walter Schloss

  • Inspired by this quote, the research team at the Department of Management and Commerce, Sri Sathya Sai Institute of Higher Learning, Andhra Pradesh, India, set out to understand the role of psychological factors and their influence on individuals' financial decision-making. The study examines the hidden relationships between individuals' inherent behavioral nature and stock trading behavior. To help achieve this goal, we are conducting an online survey coupled with a stock market simulator to gain essential insights and feedback from individual investors and finance professionals.

  • This study requires you to answer all the survey questions and participate in a simulated stock market scenario. It should take about 10 -15 minutes of your time to complete this entire exercise.

  • The survey questions are self-explanatory and straightforward, and the instructions for the stock market exercise are provided after completing the survey.

  • After completing each section, you must click on the NEXT button to proceed to the next section.

  • At the end of the stock market simulation, you need to click on the SAVE button, to ensure that all your responses are recorded.

Your responses are highly valuable to us, and we will maintain the confidentiality of your responses. The data obtained will be used for academic purposes only. If you are interested in the outcome of the study you can key in your email id (optional) in the survey and we will share the results with you.
