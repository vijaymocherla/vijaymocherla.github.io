About me 

What I do?

I'm an aspiring physicist currently looking for Ph.D. positions to work on research problems at the interface of condensed matter and AMO physics. I am interested in studying how interactions between a collection of particles give rise to remarkable emergent phenomena, with a motivation to develop applications for quantum information science. My goal is to draw upon my interdisciplinary background to harness the power of quantum computation in addressing the fundamental challenges in Physics and Chemistry.

The Back Story

I received my Bachelor of Science (Honors) in Chemistry from the Sri Sathya Sai Institute of Higher Learning in 2016. After that, I volunteered to teach at local schools before going back to college to study for a Master's degree at my Alma mater.

During the first year of my M.Sc., I took up a learning project with Prof. Sai Sathish Ramamurthy, who worked on developing low-cost plasmonic biosensors. In the summer of that year, I got a chance to continue working in the field of plasmonics with Prof. Venu Gopal Achanta at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research(TIFR) through the Visiting Student Research Program(VSRP) fellowship. While I was working on experiments to fabricate optical nanostructures during my internship, I also got introduced to the idea of playing with simple theoretical models. This motivated me to work on an independent project to develop theoretical models to study exciton transport in conjugated polymers for my master's thesis.

Soon after my graduation, I spent the summer working with Prof. Upendra Harbola at the Indian Institute of Science to look at transport models from the perspective of discrete-time random walks.
